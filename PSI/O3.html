About

Ozone in the stratosphere protects life on Earth from ultraviolet radiation. But near the ground, it is a secondary pollutant. It is formed when primary pollutants such as car emissions react with sunlight, emissions from factories and power plants etc.

Danger

Ozone damages the lungs and has been linked to asthma exacerbation and other lung problems. Ozone causes lung inflammation and makes people more susceptible to infections such as pneumonia and asthma. It also damages crops, trees and other vegetation. It is a main ingredient of urban smog.

Reducing ground level ozone

Vehiches emit large amounts of ground level ozone, try to take public transport or walk to reduce your ozone emissions!
